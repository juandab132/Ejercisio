Pasto


Pasto is a city in western Colombia, known for its Carnival of Blacks and Whites. The Carnival Museum houses giant figures used in the celebration parades. The colonial, Moorish-influenced Temple of San Juan Bautista sits next to the central Plaza Nariño. The Gold Museum of the Banco de la República displays precious pre-Columbian artifacts. Southwest of Pasto is the Santuario Las Lajas, a Gothic church built into a ravine.
